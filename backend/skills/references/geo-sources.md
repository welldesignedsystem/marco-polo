# GEO Sources: Getting Cited in AI Engines

AI answer engines (ChatGPT Browse, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Bing Copilot) pull from specific types of sources. This guide maps which platforms matter most for each engine.

---

## How AI Engines Select Sources

AI engines prioritize sources that are:
1. **Authoritative** — cited by other trusted sources
2. **Structured** — clearly organized, machine-parseable
3. **Fresh** — recently updated or published
4. **Consensus-matching** — align with what other reputable sources say
5. **Direct** — answer the question without excessive padding

---

## Platform-by-Platform GEO Guide

### Reddit

**Why it matters**: Heavily indexed by Perplexity, ChatGPT Browse, and Gemini. Reddit threads appear in AI answers more than almost any other UGC source.

**How to use it**:
- Participate genuinely in relevant subreddits (r/[yourniche], r/[topic])
- Post thorough, helpful answers to questions in your domain
- Create posts that address common queries in your industry
- Include your brand/site naturally (not spammy links)
- Upvoted, award-winning comments rank higher in AI pulls

**Best subreddits strategy**: Find the top 5 subreddits for your topic. Become a recognized contributor. AI engines weight highly-upvoted Reddit comments.

---

### Quora

**Why it matters**: Still indexed heavily by Perplexity and Google AI Overviews. Structured Q&A format is ideal for AI extraction.

**How to use it**:
- Create a complete profile with credentials (expertise signals matter)
- Answer questions in your niche with long, well-structured responses
- Use headers, bullets, and bold text in your answers
- Include a brief author bio mention where relevant
- Link back to your site's authoritative content (1 link per answer max)

---

### Wikipedia / Wikidata

**Why it matters**: The single most-cited source across all AI engines. If your brand, concept, or topic has a Wikipedia article, it dramatically increases AI citation probability.

**How to use it**:
- If your business/brand qualifies for notability, create/maintain a Wikipedia article
- Ensure your brand is mentioned (with citations) in relevant Wikipedia articles
- Add your brand to Wikidata as an entity
- Get cited in Wikipedia's external links or references sections

**Notability threshold**: Wikipedia requires "significant coverage in reliable, independent sources." Build media mentions first.

---

### LinkedIn Articles & Posts

**Why it matters**: Perplexity and Gemini increasingly pull from LinkedIn for professional/B2B topics.

**How to use it**:
- Publish long-form LinkedIn Articles (not just posts) on your topic
- Include your target query phrase in the article title
- Use a structured format: subheadings, numbered lists
- Aim for 800–2000 words
- Engage with comments to boost visibility signals

---

### Medium

**Why it matters**: Medium's domain authority (DA 95+) makes it a trusted source for AI engines. Articles here get cited frequently.

**How to use it**:
- Publish on Medium (or your brand's Medium publication)
- Include canonical link back to your main site if duplicating content
- Focus on in-depth, well-cited articles
- Use the Medium Partner Program tags for discoverability

---

### News / Media Mentions

**Why it matters**: AI engines heavily weight coverage from established news outlets (BBC, Reuters, Forbes, TechCrunch, niche trade publications).

**How to get covered**:
- Publish original research or data studies your industry cares about
- Issue press releases through PR Newswire, BusinessWire
- Use HARO (Help A Reporter Out) / Qwoted / Source Bottle to get expert quotes in articles
- Pitch journalists directly with exclusive data angles

---

### Industry Wikis & Knowledge Bases

**Why it matters**: Niche authority sites (e.g., SEMrush Glossary, HubSpot Knowledge Base, Investopedia for finance) are frequently cited by AI engines for definitional queries.

**How to use it**:
- Identify the 2–3 authoritative "wikis" in your niche
- Get your brand/content mentioned or linked from them
- Create your own glossary/knowledge base on your site (these get crawled and cited)

---

### YouTube

**Why it matters**: Google AI Overviews pull YouTube videos, especially for "how to" queries. Perplexity also surfaces video content.

**How to optimize**:
- Title: include the exact target query
- Description: write a 300+ word description with the answer in text
- Chapters/timestamps: label key sections (AI extracts from these)
- Say the answer clearly in the first 60 seconds
- Add a full transcript

---

### Your Own Site (The Foundation)

AI engines still need to be able to crawl and trust your own website. Ensure:

- **Robots.txt**: doesn't block AI crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Googlebot)
- **Sitemap**: up to date and submitted
- **Structured data**: FAQ, Article, HowTo schema
- **Direct answers**: clear, citable paragraphs at the top of key sections
- **About/Author pages**: establish who you are and your expertise

**Check your robots.txt** — many sites accidentally block AI crawlers:
```
# To ALLOW AI crawlers (remove any Disallow lines for these):
User-agent: GPTBot
User-agent: PerplexityBot
User-agent: Google-Extended
User-agent: anthropic-ai
```

## Content Types That Get Cited Most

Ranked by frequency of AI citation:

1. **Definitions** — "X is a type of Y that does Z"
2. **Statistics / Data** — "According to [study], X% of..."
3. **Step-by-step processes** — Numbered lists
4. **Comparisons** — "X vs Y: [table or clear structure]"
5. **Expert quotes** — Named expert + credential + statement
6. **Original research** — Surveys, studies, proprietary data

## Monitoring Your GEO Performance

Check if you're being cited:
- Search your target queries in **Perplexity** — are you in the sources panel?
- Search in **ChatGPT with Browse** — does your brand appear?
- Use **Google AI Overviews** — does your content get pulled?
- Set up **Google Alerts** for your brand + topic
- Use tools like **Semrush**, **Ahrefs**, or **BrightEdge** for AI visibility tracking (emerging feature category)
